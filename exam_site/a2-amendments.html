A2-1 Question 1 of 32

Which of the following choices BEST helps information owners to properly classify data?

A Understanding of technical controls that protect data
B Training on enterprise policies and standards
C Use of an automated data leak prevention tool
D Understanding which people need to access the data
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanations

A While understanding how the data are protected is important, technical controls are only effective if the data classification schema is properly understood and applied.
B Training on enterprise policies and standards, including the data classification schema, is essential for data owners or custodians. This training ensures they understand how to classify data properly according to the organization's policies and standards. Therefore, training on enterprise policies and standards is the best way to help information owners properly classify data.
C An automated data leak prevention (DLP) tool can enhance productivity and security, but it cannot replace the need for data owners to understand and apply the correct classification schema.
D Understanding who needs to access the data is important for protecting data, but it is secondary to understanding the data classification schema. Without proper classification, inappropriate access to sensitive data may be granted.
A2-2 Question 2 of 32

During a risk assessment of a healthcare organization's information systems regarding protected health information (PHI), an IS auditor interviews the IS management team. Among the findings from these interviews, which one should concern the IS auditor the most?

A Not all outgoing email messages from the organization are encrypted.
B Staff members must manually type "[PHI]" in the subject line of email messages for them to be encrypted.
C Individual computer screen saver functions are disabled.
D Server configurations require users to change their passwords once a year.
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanations

A Encrypting all outgoing email can be costly and is not a common practice across businesses.
B Relying on staff to manually indicate emails containing PHI introduces the risk of human error, as they could forget to insert the necessary keyword. It's better for the organization to implement automated encryption for outgoing emails that might contain PHI to safeguard sensitive information.
C Disabling screen savers could expose sensitive data to internal personnel, but this risk is not as significant as the potential exposure to unauthorized external parties.
D Although requiring annual password changes is a concern, it poses less risk compared to the exposure of data to unauthorized external individuals.

C Completeness of the data.

Therefore, the purpose of masking the data is to ensure the confidentiality of the data.
B Masking does not ensure the accuracy of the data. If the underlying data are inaccurate, the masked data will also be inaccurate.
C Masking does not ensure the completeness of the data. If the underlying data are incomplete, the masked data will also be incomplete.
D Masking does not ensure the reliability of the data. If the underlying data are unreliable, the masked data will also be unreliable.

A2-16 Question 16 of 32

When an organization’s disaster recovery plan (DRP) has a reciprocal agreement, which of the following risk treatment approaches is being applied?

A Transfer
B Mitigation
C Avoidance
D Acceptance
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanations

A Risk transfer involves transferring the risk to a third party, such as buying insurance for activities that pose a risk.
B A reciprocal agreement, in which two organizations agree to provide computing resources to each other in the event of a disaster, is a form of risk mitigation. This agreement helps reduce the impact of a disaster by ensuring that both organizations have backup resources available, thereby mitigating the risk. Therefore, when an organization’s disaster recovery plan includes a reciprocal agreement, it is applying a risk mitigation approach. This strategy helps ensure that the organization can continue operations in the event of a disaster by leveraging resources from another organization.
C Risk avoidance involves ceasing operations or activities that give rise to a risk. For example, an organization might stop accepting credit card payments to avoid the risk of credit card information disclosure.
D Risk acceptance occurs when an organization decides to accept the risk as it is and takes no action to mitigate or transfer it.

A2-31 Question 31 of 32

Which of the following BEST describes the function of control self- assessment?

A Quality control
B Quality assessment
C Quality planning
D Quality assurance (QA)
Correct Answer: Option D

Explanations

A Quality control focuses on fulfilling quality requirements by monitoring and evaluating the resulting service or product.
B Quality assessment involves data collection and analysis to determine the degree of conformity to predetermined standards and criteria.
C Quality planning involves designing a process or service that can meet established goals under operating conditions.
D Quality assurance (QA) is often achieved by the information systems (IS) auditor serving as a consultant, advising, and facilitating business areas to participate in process improvement and control. One such approach is through the process of control self-assessment, where business areas evaluate the effectiveness of their own controls and identify areas for improvement. Therefore, the function of control self-assessment is best described as quality assurance (QA). This process helps ensure that business areas are actively involved in evaluating and improving their own controls, contributing to the overall quality and effectiveness of the organization's processes.

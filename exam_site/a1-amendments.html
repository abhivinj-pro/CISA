A1-1 Question 1 of 40

Which of the following BEST represents an ethical responsibility of project auditors?

A Auditors are permitted to divulge proprietary information to which they might have access to support the auditee’s goals.
B Auditors should discuss the outcomes of previous audit exercises conducted elsewhere during the audit to ensure completeness.
C Audit team leaders must demonstrate fairness when settling disputes among members.
D Unproven facts should be reported by the auditors to ensure further investigation.
Correct Answer: Option C

Explanations

A Auditors are not permitted to divulge proprietary information. Such disclosure jeopardizes trust and damages the working relationship between the auditor and auditee.
B Auditors must avoid discussing the outcomes of previous audit exercises conducted elsewhere, except in cases where such information is directly relevant and applicable, to maintain confidentiality and independence.
C If an issue causes a dispute among audit team members, it should be investigated without prejudice to keep the project on course. Audit team leaders must demonstrate fairness and objectivity in resolving disputes to ensure the integrity and credibility of the audit process. Therefore, the best representation of an ethical responsibility of project auditors is that audit team leaders must demonstrate fairness when settling disputes among members. This ensures the integrity, objectivity, and reliability of the audit process.
D Unproven facts should not be reported by the auditors. Their findings should be disclosed to responsible personnel to test the validity of the audit findings before formally documenting them in the audit report.

A Prevent unauthorized disclosure of organizational data.
B Ensure that organizational objectives for separation of duties are met.
C Minimize the impact of an incident.
D Ensure the continuity of business after any disaster.
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanations

A While the access control policy does help prevent unauthorized disclosure of data, this is more directly related to confidentiality principles.
B The primary purpose of reviewing the access control policy is to ensure that organizational objectives for separation of duties are met. This helps prevent fraud and errors by ensuring that no single individual has control over all aspects of any critical process, thereby minimizing the risk of unauthorized access and ensuring policy requirements are adhered to. Therefore, the IS auditor should review the access control policy primarily to ensure that organizational objectives for separation of duties are met. This helps maintain proper internal controls and reduces the risk of unauthorized actions within the organization.
C The access control policy helps minimize errors but is not primarily designed to minimize the impact of an incident.
D Ensuring business continuity after a disaster is the concern of the business continuity policy, not the access control policy.

A1-7 Question 7 of 40

What BEST describes the risk that information collected may contain a material error that may go undetected during information systems (IS) auditing?

A Inherent risk
B Audit risk
C Control risk
D Detection risk
Correct Answer: Option B

Explanations

A Inherent risk is the risk level or exposure of the process/entity to be audited without considering the controls that management has implemented. It represents the susceptibility of an area to material misstatement before considering any related controls.
B Audit risk is the probability that information or financial reports may contain material errors and that the auditor may not detect an error that has occurred. It encompasses both the risk of material misstatement and the risk that the auditor will not detect such errors. Therefore, audit risk best describes the risk that information collected may contain a material error that may go undetected during information systems (IS) auditing. This risk includes inherent risk, control risk, and detection risk.
C Control risk is the risk that a material error exists that would not be prevented or detected on a timely basis by the system of internal controls. It is one component of audit risk.
D Detection risk is the risk that material errors or misstatements that have occurred will not be detected by an information systems (IS) auditor. It is another component of audit risk.

A1-11 Question 11 of 40

An IT auditor reviewed the transactions log of an audit engagement partner and discovered some suspicious activity, which may be interpreted as potential fraud. However, the auditor was not able to determine the circumstances around the incidents or obtain further evidence. The auditor decided to disclose this information in case there are questions in the audit quality assurance review. In taking this action, the auditor has:

A Violated auditing standards because the auditor should inform the appropriate authorities/management of the suspected fraud.
B Violated laws because unlawful activities should have been reported to the appropriate regulatory agency.
C Not violated auditing standards because the auditor has committed to disclose the facts, when required.
D Not violated auditing standards because there is a lack of evidence as to whether a fraud has been committed or not.
Correct Answer: Option A

Explanations

A In situations where auditors are not able to obtain sufficient or appropriate audit evidence necessary to achieve the audit objectives, they should disclose the situation to audit management and, if necessary, to those charged with audit governance, as per established procedures. Restrictions or limitations on the scope of the audit and achievement of the audit objectives should also be disclosed in the communication of the audit results. The auditor should inform the appropriate authorities within the enterprise if the indicators of fraud are sufficient to recommend an investigation. Thus, the auditor has a duty to act even though the available facts do not conclusively prove that an irregularity has occurred. Auditors should disclose all material facts and findings known to them that, if not disclosed, may distort the reporting of activities under review. Therefore, the correct course of action would be for the auditor to inform the appropriate authorities/management of the suspected fraud, even if further evidence is not available. This ensures that the necessary steps are taken to investigate and address the potential issue.
B Unlawful activities should not be reported to outside entities until there is sufficient evidence. However, the suspicion of these activities should be disclosed to appropriate executives within the enterprise.
C While the auditor must report the results of audit work, simply committing to disclose facts when required is not sufficient action in this context. The auditor must take active steps to communicate the suspicion to the appropriate authorities.
D Limitations on the achievement of audit objectives should be disclosed in the audit results and reporting, and a possible irregularity should be disclosed to the appropriate enterprise authorities. However, the auditor should also inform the appropriate internal authorities about the potential fraud.
